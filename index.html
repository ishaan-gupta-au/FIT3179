Global Impact of Food on Greenhouse Gas Emissions

The Earth has experienced rapid climate change in the recent years. Though we may not often think about what we eat, food has a major carbon footprint, accounting for over a quarter of greenhouse gas emissions across the globe. We wish to explore this impact in further detail and identify the animals that impact the environment most.

Share of Global Greenhouse Gas Emissions From Food

The adjacent graph illustrates the global share of greenhouse gas emissions caused by food. Of all the countries, China occupies the highest share of emissions, followed by the U.S.A, Indonesia, Brazil and India.

Carbon Emissions Per Kilogram of Food

Generally, animals release the most carbon emissions in the atmosphere, with beef herds generating as much as 100 kilograms of carbon dioxide per kilogram of meat. Though chocolate and coffee do release high amounts of carbon dioxide, these foods have a small marginal effect on greenhouse gas emissions given their production size.
